# What is Quantum Computing?

## Introduction

Quantum computing is a new type of computing that uses the principles of **quantum physics** to process information.  
Unlike classical computers that use **bits (0 or 1)**, quantum computers use **qubits**, which can exist in multiple states at the same time.

Because of this, quantum computers may be able to solve certain complex problems much faster than traditional computers.

---

## Classical Computing vs Quantum Computing

| Classical Computing | Quantum Computing |
|---------------------|------------------|
| Uses bits (0 or 1) | Uses qubits |
| Processes information sequentially | Can explore many possibilities at once |
| Based on classical physics | Based on quantum mechanics |

---

## Basic Idea of a Qubit

![Bloch Sphere Representation](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Bloch_sphere.svg/512px-Bloch_sphere.svg.png)

A **qubit** is the basic unit of quantum information.  
Unlike a classical bit, a qubit can represent **0, 1, or a combination of both states simultaneously**.

This property is called **superposition**.

---

## Important Concepts

### Superposition
Superposition allows a quantum system to exist in **multiple states at the same time**, allowing quantum computers to analyze many possibilities simultaneously.

### Entanglement
Entanglement occurs when two qubits become linked such that the state of one qubit affects the other instantly.

## Potential Applications

Quantum computing could help solve problems in areas such as:

- Drug discovery  
- Cryptography  
- Optimization problems  
- Climate modeling  
- Artificial intelligence  

---

## Current Challenges

Quantum computers are still in the early stages of development and face several challenges:

- Qubit instability  
- High error rates  
- Expensive hardware  
- Need for extremely low operating temperatures  

Because of these limitations, classical computers are still used for most computing tasks today.

---

## Conclusion

Quantum computing represents a promising new approach to computation based on the laws of quantum mechanics. While still experimental, it has the potential to solve problems that are currently very difficult for classical computers.
